Yakimeshi

Home

Description:

This easy and flavorful fried rice is packed with veggies, protein, and savory seasoning. Whether you use shrimp or pork, the dish comes together quickly and makes a perfect weeknight meal. Chilled rice, sesame oil, and soy sauce give it that classic takeout flavor, while eggs and colorful vegetables add texture and nutrition.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons neutral oil, divided
  • 2 large eggs, beaten to blend
  • 5 oz medium shrimp, peeled, deveined, cut into ¼-inch pieces
    or 3 oz Japanese Berkshire pork sausage
  • 1 large carrot, finely chopped (about ¾ cup)
  • 2 scallions, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 3 cups cooked short-grain rice or brown rice, chilled
  • ¾ cup corn (canned or frozen, thawed)
  • ¾ cup peas (canned or frozen, thawed)
  • ½ teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Kosher salt (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add 1 tbsp neutral oil and the eggs. Cook, stirring constantly, for 2-3 minutes until just cooked. Transfer to a plate.
  2. In the same pan, add the remaining 2 tbsp neutral oil. Once hot, add shrimp (or pork), carrot, scallions, and garlic. Stir and cook for about 2 minutes until the carrot softens and darkens in color.
  3. Add the rice. Stir and break it apart, mixing it with the rest of the ingredients. Cook for about 1 minute until warmed through.
  4. Add the corn, peas, and white pepper. Mix well.
  5. Push the rice to one side. Pour the sesame oil into the empty side. Once hot, stir everything together.
  6. Push the rice aside again. Add soy sauce to the empty side. Let it sizzle, then stir it into the rice.
  7. Add the reserved cooked eggs. Mix well and serve immediately.
